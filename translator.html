About Braille

What is Braille?

Braille is a tactile writing system used by people who are visually impaired. It consists of patterns of raised dots arranged in cells.

How it works

Each Braille character is made up of six dots arranged in two columns of three dots each. Different combinations represent letters, numbers, and punctuation.

Louis Braille

Created by Louis Braille in 1824 when he was just 15 years old. This system revolutionized reading and writing for the blind community.
